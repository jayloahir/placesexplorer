1.Aamby Valley

Aamby Valley is a beautiful township about 24 kilometres from Lonavala, in the district of Pune in Maharashtra, India.
It has been developed by Sahara India Parivar.

The 10,000-acre hilly terrain that the township now occupies has been converted into a splendid landscape through designs that are best suited for the environment and have an urban infrastructure compatible with international standards. The township is frequented for a day-long picnic or a weekend getaway by manybr.

The luxurious urban infrastructure, the tranquil ambience and exclusive privileges at this exotic location attract tourists from all over the world for various activities including skydiving.

2.Bir,Himachal Pradesh

Bir-Billing is considered as one of the top three paragliding sites in the world. Known as the Paragliding capital of India, Bir attracts adventure-seekers from throughout the country and the world.

Hence, some of the best time that you might spend here might be with your feet in the air. As exciting and thrilling as it is, the activity of paragliding is set amid the lush green meadows with starting point at Billing (2400 m) and landing takes place in the fields at Western Edge of Tibetan Colony in Chowgan (Bir, towards 14 km south).

While the experienced get to do a solo show, a novice gets an adventure full ride in a two-seater glider with a skilled instructor.Bir-Billing is undoubtedly one of the most adventurous places in India.

3.Rishikesh,Uttarakhand

Located in the foothills of the Himalayas along the convergence of Ganga and Chandrabhaga River, Rishikesh (also called as Hrishikesh) is known for its adventure activities, ancient temples, popular cafes and as the "Yoga Capital of the World".

Rishikesh is a brilliant spot for white water rafting. Other adventure activities include mountain biking, canyoning and even bungee jumping. <>

Rishikesh is also known for its connection with The Beatles. In February 1968, members of the legendary English rock band visited Maharishi Mahesh Yogi's ashram (now popularly known as the Beatles Ashram) to learn transcendental meditation.
The band composed nearly 48 songs during their time at the Maharishi's ashram, many of which appear on the White Album (and a few from Abbey Road).
John Lennon recorded a song titled, 'The Happy Rishikesh Song' after his visit.
Several other international artists, including Mike Love of the Beach Boys, Paul Horn, Donovan and Gyp Mills, visited the site to contemplate and meditate.

4.Ghumli,Gujarat

Navlakha Temple, Ghumli

Government of Gujarat and Archaeological Survey of India have taken a project to restore historical places of Ghumli.
At present, Ghumli is an important archaeological site of Gujarat: The point of interest are - Navlakha temple; Ghumli Ganesh temple; Ashapura temple; Vindyavasini temple; Sonkansari or Son Kasari temple; step-wells [Vikai or Vikia Vav and Jetha vav]; historical gates.
Kileshwar Mahadev temple, built by Jethwa rulers is nearby. Bhan Gate named after Bhan Jethwa near Ghumali Navlakha Temple and Rampol Gate at Ghumali are of historical interest.
Trikamji Bapu Mandir, Ravno Nes and so is Khodiyar mataji jar are other places of interest.

Ghumli houses Navlakha Temple, built by Jethwa rulers in the 12th century, considered to be oldest Sun temple of Gujarat, now in ruins.
The temple is built in the Solanki style of architecture with entwining tusk of three elephants as a trade mark of temple. It is located at the foothills.
Close by, little easterly to Navlakha temple there are step wells called "Vikai Vav" the largest in Kathiawar and "Jetha vav".
Vikai or Vikia Vaav built by Jethwa ruler Vikiaji after whom it is named, is the oldest and one of the biggest step wells of Gujarat measuring almost 60 by 40.5 sq m. The well has numerous flights of steps leading up to it and string-coursed carvings.
The entrance pavilions can still be seen standing intact at three places.

A motorcycle trip to Leh Ladakh is the ultimate challenge for adventurous bikers.

Satiate your adventure lust on a trip to Ladakh as it is considered one of the best road trips in India by bike.

The popularity of this adventure place in India has led to the development of a number of strong biking communities that organize bike trips to Leh-Ladakh every year.

6.Thekkady

Bamboo Rafting in Thekkady

Bamboo Rafting in India has been practiced for ages. Earlier people used to get from one place to the other on a bamboo raft.
Thekkady Tourism has played a vital role in promoting this eco-friendly, entertainment program for nature lovers.
Bamboo Rafting in Thekkady booking must be done in advance and then only you can enjoy a fun ride at one of the best adventure places in South India, Kerala.
It is a full-day activity starting from morning 8 am to 5 pm in the evening.
Tourists are accompanied by well-armed forest keepers and four guides.
These guides are mostly from tribal communities who are familiar with every nook and corner of these thick forests.
This 3-hour Thekkady Boating is one of the best things to do in Thekkady, where you can witness panoramic views of forest-clad hills along with animals like elephants, gaur, and sambar.

8.Badami

Rock Climbing in Badami

Owing to the presence of red sandstone cliffs that encircle the Agastya Tirtha water reservoir, Badami is a great adventure destination in Karnataka.
In addition, there are small cracks on these cliffs, which makes Rock Climbing at Badami perfect for rock climbers.
Free climbing, bouldering, and rappelling are the three different kinds of rock climbs in Badami you can try.
Apart from this, Trekking in Badami is also a popular activity.
Many adventurous rock climbers including both beginners and experts, can enjoy a visit to Badami to satiate their adrenaline.
These climbs are graded from 5B to 8B+ and require 2-4 hours depending on complexity, team size, weather, and comfort level of participants. Not to mention, the ease of rock climbing at Badami has earned it the title of ‘the Mecca of Rock Climbing’ among locals and international rock climbing aficionados

9.Andaman

Scuba Diving in Andaman

The crystal clear waters of Andaman ensure clear vision for divers.
Through scuba diving, one can witness the rich marine life – small reef fish, stingrays, fragile coral ecosystems, and reef sharks and turtles.
Andaman is one of the best places for scuba diving in India but if you do not know how to dive, there are many scuba diving courses in Andaman for non-swimmers.
Learn scuba diving in Andaman with the best instructors and become an expert diver.
The certificates you get from PADI/SSI are valid throughout the globe.
After completion, you can dive at most parts of the globe with the diving card you get after you have cleared the certification.
Apart from this, Snorkeling in Andaman is also a great adventure sport you can try.
The age limit for Scuba Diving in Andaman is between 10 to 60 years and people with high or low hypertension, heart patients, and pregnant women are not advised to go underwater.

You kids are going to enjoy riding this beast and have a wonderful time.
Desert camps in Jaisalmer are also organized to get an authentic experience with traditional folk music and Rajasthani food.
Camel safaris in Jaisalmer’s Camel safaris in Jaisalmer’s Thar Desert is a fun activity to explore the golden sand dunes of Rajasthan.
The desert safari tours range from a few hours to a couple of weeks.
Not only in Jaisalmer but desert safaris in Rajasthan are popular in the sand dunes of Osian, Jodhpur, Bikaner, and Pushkar.
