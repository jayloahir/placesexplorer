TOP CITYS

1.DELHI

Delhi, the capital city of the country, is the largest city in India. It is located in northern India and has been the capital of India since 1931 tracing a long history as a hub of power and governance in the country.
As per the latest census of 2011, Delhi's metropolitan region has a population of over 25 million, making it the world's second largest urban area after Tokyo.
Spread across an area of 1,484 square kilometers, Delhi is administered by the Delhi Municipal Corporation. Under a single authority, it is the largest city by area in India..

2.MUMBAI

Mumbai, city, capital of Maharashtra state, southwestern India.
It is the country’s financial and commercial centre and its principal port on the Arabian Sea..

Located on Maharashtra’s coast, Mumbai is India’s most-populous city, andit is one of the largest and most densely populated urban areas in the world.

It was built on a site of ancient settlement, and it took its name from the local goddess Mumba—a form of Parvati, the consort of Shiva, one of the principal deities of Hinduism—whose temple once stood in what is now the southeastern section of the city.

It became known as Bombay during the British colonial period, the name possibly an Anglicized corruption of Mumbai or perhaps of Bom Baim (“Good Harbour”), supposedly a Portuguese name for the locale.
The name Mumbai was restored officially in 1995, although Bombay remained in common usage.

3.BENGALURU

Bengaluru, also known as Bangalore, is the second largest city in India.
It is the capital of Karnataka state. Bengaluru is a major economic hub and is known as the Silicon Valley of India due to its dominance in the IT sector.
Many major tech companies like Infosys, Wipro, Microsoft etc have offices in Bengaluru.

The city has a nice climate throughout the year which is neither too hot nor too cold.
It has a cosmopolitan culture and people from all over India have made it their home. Bengaluru is well-connected via air, rail and road to other cities.

It has a well-developed infrastructure in terms of housing, healthcare, education etc. The city has lively pubs, clubs and restaurants serving a variety of cuisines.
Bengaluru is a major tourist attraction with places like Lalbagh Botanical Garden, Cubbon Park and Tipu Sultan's Summer Palace..

4.HYDRABAD

Hyderabad is the third largest and capital city of Telangana state.
It was established in 1591 CE and has a rich cultural heritage. Hyderabad used to be the capital of the princely state of Hyderabad during the rule of Nizams.

The city has a mixture of Mughal and indigenous architecture with buildings like Charminar, Golconda Fort and Qutub Shahi Tombs attracting tourists.
Hyderabad is now a major IT hub generating about $35 billion in export revenue annually. Many pharma and biotech companies are based in Hyderabad.

The city has a pleasant climate throughout the year and attracts migrants from various parts of India. Hyderabad is known for its unique culture and cuisine which is a blend of the Nizams' Arabian tastes and local Telugu flavors.
Biryani is the most popular dish here besides other Mughlai dishes..

5.AHEMDAVAD

Ahmedabad, located in Gujarat is the largest municipality of India.

It was founded by Sultan Ahmed Shah in 1411 and is an important economic and industrial center.
The city is well known for its architectural heritage with buildings exhibiting Indo-Saracenic style like Siddi Sayed’s Mosque and Teen Darwaza. Ahmedabad is home to Gandhi Ashram established by Mahatma Gandhi and adjoining Kankaria Lake which is a favorite picnic spot.

It has emerged as a major manufacturing hub especially in pharmaceutical, chemical and textile industries.
The city boasts a lively cultural life with annual festivals like Uttarayan kite festival and Dakor Dance celebrating cultural diversity..

6.PUNE

Pune is an important cultural and commercial center located in Maharashtra.

It emerged as a major educational hub with top universities like University of Pune, Ferguson College and Armed Forces Medical College.
Pune is home to many national defense institutions employing thousands. The city experienced a huge IT and automobile boom to emerge as an industrial powerhouse.
Some major companies located here include Tata Motors, Mercedes Benz, Kinetic Engineering etc. Pune has a pleasant climate throughout the year making it ideal for living.

It also has a vibrant cultural scene and is known for its street food like vada pav and misal..

7.SURAT

Surat is located in south Gujarat and is one of the fastest growing cities in India.

It has emerged as a diamond cutting and polishing center and hosts over 90% of the world's diamond cutting.
Surat's banking, finance and textile industries also contribute to its rapid growth. The city has a cosmopolitan culture with people from different parts of India settling here. Surat has a warm & humid climate throughout the year. Major attractions include Dutch Cemetery - centuries old burial ground, and Surat Castle built during British rule. Surat is well-connected nationally and internationally. An efficient BRTS system helps in commuting within the city.

Constructions of smart roads and new international airport are working towards transforming Surat into a smart city.

8.INDORE

Indore is the largest and most populous city of Madhya Pradesh. It is recognized as one of India's cleanest, greenest and most developed city and was awarded the title "India's Best City" in 2016. Indore has emerged as a major commercial center and is known as the financial capital of Madhya Pradesh.

It has a strong base in industries like automobiles, electronics and food processing. The city has state of the art infrastructure and civic services.
It is well-planned with wide roads and parks like Lal Bagh and Rajwada Palace attracting many visitors.

Indore is also an educational hub with top universities, colleges and institutes.

9.BHOPAL

Bhopal is the capital city of Madhya Pradesh located near the middle of India. In the 6th century, Bhopal was ruled by the Hindu kings and it came under the Delhi Sultanate in the 14th century and later under the Mughals.

The city has a very rich culture and heritage in terms of monuments, arts and crafts.
Bhopal gained prominence during the rule of the Muslim qawwal (partially literate) or the "Nawabs of Bhopal".

During their rule, many mosques and monuments were built. The most striking example is the Moti Masjid or the famous pearl mosque.
Bhopal is currently a center for trade and commerce with many factories and industrial units.

However, the city was once affected by the world's worst industrial disaster, the Bhopal gas tragedy of 1984.

10.LUCKNOW

Lucknow, the capital city of Uttar Pradesh is the fifth largest urban agglomeration in India.

It was previously the capital of the Awadh region under the Nawabs.

Lucknow has a very rich cultural heritage and is known for its tehzeeb or refined culture.
The city has splendid monumental architecture like the Bara Imambara, Rumi Darwaza and Chota Imambara from the Nawabi era. Lucknow is known for its handicrafts especially chikankari embroidery and is famous for its cuisine.

Lucknowi biryani and kebabs are considered among the finest in India. As the administrative capital of UP, Lucknow is a major educational hub with many colleges and universities.
It has emerged as an important textile industry centre as well and is well connected by air, roads and rail to other Indian cities.
